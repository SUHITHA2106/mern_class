"The Dark Knight" (2008), directed by Christopher Nolan, is the critically acclaimed second film in Nolan's Batman trilogy. The story centers around Batman (Christian Bale) facing his greatest challenge: the chaos unleashed by the Joker (Heath Ledger), a criminal mastermind who seeks to plunge Gotham City into anarchy. The film explores themes of morality, chaos versus order, and heroism. Batman, with the help of District Attorney Harvey Dent (Aaron Eckhart) and Lieutenant Jim Gordon (Gary Oldman), struggles to maintain peace and justice amid the Joker's ruthless plans. The Joker's schemes threaten to destroy everything, forcing Batman to make difficult moral choices. Heath Ledger's portrayal of the Joker received widespread praise and earned him a posthumous Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. The movie is known for its intense action sequences, complex characters, and thought-provoking narrative, making it a standout in superhero cinema. The Dark Knight is a 2008 superhero film directed by Christopher Nolan, from a screenplay co-written with his brother Jonathan. Based on the DC Comics superhero Batman, it is the sequel to Batman Begins (2005), and the second installment in The Dark Knight trilogy. The plot follows the vigilante Batman, police lieutenant James Gordon, and district attorney Harvey Dent, who form an alliance to dismantle organized crime in Gotham City. Their efforts are derailed by the Joker, an anarchistic mastermind who seeks to test how far Batman will go to save the city from chaos.
